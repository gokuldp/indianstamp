Commemorative Stamp

A commemorative stamp is a type of postage stamp that is issued for everyday use, as opposed to commemorative stamps, which are issued to mark a specific event or anniversary. commemorative stamps typically feature a country's coat of arms, national symbols, or iconic landmarks. They are usually printed in large quantities and are available for purchase at post offices for an extended period.

Characteristics of commemorative stamps:

  1. Regular issue: commemorative stamps are issued for regular postal use. .
  2. Long-term availability: They are available for purchase for an extended period, often several years.
  3. Large print runs: commemorative stamps are printed in large quantities to meet ongoing postal demands
  4. IStandard designs: They typically feature standard designs, such as national symbols or iconic landmarks.

Examples of commemorative stamps include:

  1. The United States' American Flag stamp
  2. The United Kingdom's Machin series, featuring Queen Elizabeth I
  3. Canada's Maple Leaf stamp
