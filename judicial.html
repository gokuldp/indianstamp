Judicial stamps

Judicial stamps, also known as court fee stamps, are a type of stamp used in India to pay fees for court services, such as filing lawsuits, appeals, and other legal documents.

Types of Judicial Stamps:

  1. Court Fee Stamps: Used to pay fees for court services, such as filing lawsuits and appeals.
  2. Revenue Stamps: Used to pay revenue-related fees, such as stamp duty on property transactions.
  3. Non-Judicial Stamps: Used for non-court-related purposes, such as notarization and authentication of documents.

Features of Judicial Stamps:

Denomination:

    Judicial stamps come in various denominations, ranging from ₹1 to ₹25,000 or more.
Design:
    The design of judicial stamps typically features the Ashoka Pillar, the national emblem of India, along with the denomination and other relevant details.
Paper:
    Judicial stamps are printed on special paper with security features to prevent counterfeiting.

Cancellation:

  1. Judicial stamps are cancelled by the court or issuing authority to prevent reuse.
