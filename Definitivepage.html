Definitive Stamp

A definitive stamp is a type of postage stamp that is issued for everyday use, as opposed to commemorative stamps, which are issued to mark a specific event or anniversary. Definitive stamps typically feature a country's coat of arms, national symbols, or iconic landmarks. They are usually printed in large quantities and are available for purchase at post offices for an extended period.

Characteristics of definitive stamps:

  1. Regular issue: Definitive stamps are issued for regular postal use. .
  2. Long-term availability: They are available for purchase for an extended period, often several years.
  3. Large print runs: Definitive stamps are printed in large quantities to meet ongoing postal demands
  4. IStandard designs: They typically feature standard designs, such as national symbols or iconic landmarks.

Examples of definitive stamps include:

  1. The United States' American Flag stamp
  2. The United Kingdom's Machin series, featuring Queen Elizabeth I
  3. Canada's Maple Leaf stamp
